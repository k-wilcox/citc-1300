History

Chattanooga, a city with a rich past, began as a Native American settlement before European settlers arrived in the late 18th century. Its strategic location along the Tennessee River and railroad lines made it vital during the Civil War, earning it the nickname "The Gateway to the Deep South." Post-war, Chattanooga thrived as an industrial center, particularly in iron and textiles. Today, the city balances its historic roots with modern innovation, boasting a vibrant downtown, preserved landmarks, and a focus on outdoor recreation and the arts.

Things to do in Chattanooga

Family Friendly!

  • Explore Ruby Falls, an underground waterfall nestled inside Lookout Mountain.
  • Take a scenic ride on the Lookout Mountain Incline Railway, the world's steepest passenger railway.
  • Visit the Tennessee Aquarium to discover diverse aquatic life from freshwater streams to ocean depths.
  • Wander through the charming streets of the Bluff View Art District, home to galleries, cafes, and stunning views of the river.
  • Embark on a leisurely stroll or bike ride along the Tennessee Riverwalk, a scenic pathway stretching for miles along the waterfront.
